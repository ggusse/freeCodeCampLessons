John von Neumann

The last great mathematician

Von Neumann's Most Notable Contributions:

  • Manhattan Project - As a regular consultant for the United States Navy, John von Neumann was ultimately asked to consult on the design of the atomic bomb. Having a background in the mathematical simulation of shaped explosive charges, Von Neumann understood the physics of deflection. This led him to be the primary proponent for the implosion-type design of the Fat Man that was used in the attack on Nagasaki.
  • Mathematics - Von Neumann was primarily known as a mathematician and has a long list of namesakes as a result. Many consider him to be the greatest mathematical mind of his time, if not all time. Von Neumann has made noteworthy contributions to set theory, ergodic theory, operator theory, measure theory, lattice theory, and geometry. He was often described as a man who had an intuition about the mathematical principles behind any new theory.
  • Physics - Some of the most important applications of Von Neumann's mathematics happened, and continue to happen in the field of quantum physics. He was a key figure in the development of the axioms that make up the mathematical framework for quantum mechanics. Von Neumann entropy is even considered to be the cornerstone of quantum information theory.
  • Computing - John von Neumann was quintessential to the field of computing in both the technical and philosophical sense. He invented the now well-known merge sort algorithm and the design architecture for the first digital computer, which is still relied upon today. He also worked with Alan Turing on the philosophy of artificial intelligence.
